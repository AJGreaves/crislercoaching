For personal matters, a one-to-one confidential coaching session welcomes you fully as you are, inviting thoughts as well as emotions as a guide to your needs, or what is most important to you. In this process the aim is inner clarity and discovery of your next steps, void of all judgment and outside advice.

A wide range of personal situations are welcome.

Some examples I help with regularly:

  • "I wish to break through old patterns that are no longer serving me - how can I do this?"
  • "I long for more meaningful connection in my life, but don't manage it yet - how can I get it?"
  • "I'm a conflict-avoider and see that this isn't serving me well - how can I build skills needed to handle confrontational situations?"
  • "I am told that I take things too personally, but don't know what to do about it - can I unlearn this?"
  • "I struggle with a harsh inner critic that limits me - what can I do about it?"
  • "I want to be clearer about my boundaries and learn how to say no in a way that doesn't break connection with people I care about."
  • "How can I better deal with my (and others') 'big emotions'?"
  • "I face big decisions, but can't seem to make up my mind - how can I better find an answer to them?"
  • "I am dealing with heavy loss and don't know how to process it - how do I mourn?"
  • "I am concerned about my well-being due to life-work balance being off - how to improve this?"
  • "I am afraid of anger - how can I better deal with it when I'm confronted with it?"
  • "I have a lot of anger in me - how can I get a better handle on it?"
  • "I am headed into a challenging conversation (potential conflict) and need preparation as to how to best go about it."

Coaching for (committed) couples

Couples sessions are for those committed to one another and seek conflict resolution,
(re-)connection and/or learning new communication skills for sustained connection.

Couples sessions are for those committed to one another AND seek conflict resolution, (re-)connection and/or learning new communication skills for sustained connection.

Typically, there is a 4-step process for maximum effectiveness:

  1. Initial, free intake conversations take place online or via telephone. These can be together or separate - please schedule using my online calendar.
  2. If you decide to work with me, I send you a draft agreement which lays out mutual clarity about how we do that. Once we each are in agreement, we sign a final agreement.
  3. I give each of you a private 30-minute online or telephone “empathy session” for full expression, being heard and inner clarity.
  4. These are followed by a joint session, in which I support you to be fully heard by each other, gain mutual clarity, understanding and compassion. Empathic listening is used as a basis to meet you both just where you are, without correcting, blaming, shaming, or solution-giving. From there new agreements are found (by you) to try out and later evaluate together and revise.

Couples can schedule as many sessions as are desired. Most go for a 5-session trajectory, with a discounted price.

Some examples I help with regularly:

  • "We wish to break through old patterns that are no longer serving us - how can we do this?"
  • "We long for more meaningful, sustained connection, yet we don't manage - how can we get it?"
  • "We fight all the time and don't manage to resolve anything - how do we do it?"
  • "We avoid all conflict and don't manage to work through our issues - how do we learn conflict resolution skills?"
  • "We don't manage to make decisions that work for us both - how to do this?"
  • "We deal with anger very differently - one fights and the other freezes or flees - how to deal with this differently?"
  • "We are interested in exploring an open relationship, but find it very difficult to talk about it - outside guidance is welcome."

Courses for existing groups

Groups who collaborate on a regular basis benefit greatly from communication skill-building training.
Courses are generally 20-30 hours and take place on a weekly basis (in person or online).

Groups who collaborate on a regular basis benefit greatly from communication skill-building training. Courses are generally 20-30 hours and take place on a weekly basis (in person or online).

If requested to help determine interest, an introductory 1-day workshop can be provided.

  • Connecting communication (based on Marshall Rosenberg's Nonviolent Communication), foundation - advanced levels
  • Giving & receiving effective feedback
  • Building safety and understanding via active listening skills
  • Letting emotions serve (balancing appreciation / celebration . . . unpleasant impact / mourning)
  • Inclusive decision-making (based on Miki Kashtan's Convergent Facilitation method)
  • Bridging differences (working with polarization)
  • Power & privilege: uncovering our blindspots
